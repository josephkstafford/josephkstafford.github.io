Users & research

Recruitment → interviews → survey → personas

Recruitment
  • Uni societies, course chats, Discord/Reddit; small gift-card raffle.
  • Mix by year (1st → Masters) and sub-discipline (mech, EEE, aero, process…).
Interview highlights
  • Eligibility is unclear: students open many roles only to find they’re not eligible.
  • Deadlines slip: no reliable reminders; reopenings often missed.
  • Sub-disciplines are hard to isolate: filters too broad elsewhere.
  • Trust: “verified apply link” repeatedly requested.
Survey (50+)
  • Validated demand for eligibility at a glance and deadline reminders.
  • Preference for a simple table UI + fast filters.
Personas (3)
  • Primary: “Alex” — deadline pain, wants constant reminders of new listings and ones closing soon.
  • Secondary: “Emily” — discovery/clarity, esp. early years.
  • “Ava” (planner) de-prioritised for V1 due to switching costs.

Market & competition

Sizing & positioning

Top-down market size (concise)
  • Total market (UK higher education): ~2,900,000 students (2023/24).
  • TAM (Engineering & Technology): 179,000 students/year × 4-year cohort ≈ 716,000.
  • SAM (likely premium users): 1.7% × 716,000 ≈ 12,200 subscribers.
  • SOM (directional revenue): 12,200 × £10 ≈ £122k/year.
Market growth (headline)
  • Direction: overall upward trend with a dip in 2023/24.
  • Planning band: base forecast is flat-to-slightly up; sensitivity = −2.85% / +1.36%.
Assumptions (for transparency)
  • 4-year rolling cohort for TAM; conservative conversion for SAM; early-stage intro pricing for SOM.
Positioning

Different because: EngTrackr focuses on eligibility clarity and timing (deadlines/reopenings) with verified links—not generic job search.

Measure & adapt

A/B test — “Save” (A) vs “Track” (B)

Tested two button labels to help people save one role quickly. This was a label-only change.

n = 12 100% success (A & B) B (“Track”) ~14% faster Adopted for V2
What we tested
  • A = “Save” (label only)
  • B = “Track” (label only)
Why

Use the clearest wording so students can spot the action quickly and save a role without thinking.

How we ran it
  • Participants: n = 12, counter-balanced order.
  • Task: “Find and save ONE role you like.”
  • Success metric: saved any role within 60 s (Y/N).
  • Secondary metric: time to first save (seconds).
  • Decision rule: if success is similar (≤10 pp), ship the faster option (≥2–4 s gain).
Results

Both labels reached 100% success. “Track” was faster to first action.

Metric A — “Save” B — “Track”
Success (≤60 s) 12/12 (100%) 12/12 (100%)
Avg. time to first save 14 s 12 s
Decision

Shipped B (“Track”) for V2. Same success, faster to first action (~2 s, ~14% quicker).

Impact

Clearer wording reduced scanning time with no drop in success. Users spotted “Track” quickly; a few hesitated on “Save.”

Risks

Risks & Mitigations

  • Finding users / awareness: start with uni societies & course chats; short YT/TikTok explainers.
  • Competitors: few UK-specific trackers; win on clarity, speed, and verified data.
  • Scam/bad links: link verification + visible “Report issue” flow.

About

From Mechanical Engineering to Product

My Mechanical Engineering degree trained me to take open-ended problems and make them workable: define clear requirements and success criteria, prototype to learn, measure what matters, and communicate decisions with a team. Those habits transfer directly to Product Management—structured thinking, evidence before opinion, and comfort working with constraints.

What I bring

  • Customer curiosity: talk to users, shadow flows, and read support threads to understand real problems.
  • Self-directed learning & prototyping: teach myself tools (Figma/HTML/CSS), make quick demos, invite feedback early.
  • Structured thinking & writing: frame problems, define outcomes/constraints, capture assumptions & risks in a 1-pager.
  • Prioritisation & trade-offs: use impact-vs-effort to pick the next smallest thing worth testing.
